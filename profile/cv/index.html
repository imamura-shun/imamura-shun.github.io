Employment

  • Special Research Fellow, Graduate School of Engineering, Department of Chemical Engineering, Kyoto University, Japan, 2021-Present
  • Research Assistant, National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology (AIST), AIST-Tohoku University Advanced Mathematical Materials Modeling Open Innovation Laboratory (MathAM-OIL), Japan, 2020-2021
    • Research Theme: Direct numerical simulations of Quincke rollers with fully resolved hydrodynamics
    • Content: Conducted direct numerical simulations to study the behavior of colloidal particles rotating on a substrate. This work demonstrated directly how fluid dynamics and electrical interactions couple, leading to complex behaviors through fluidic dissipation and electrical alignment effects.
    • Methods: Developed simulations using C++ implementing the Smoothed Profile Method to represent particles in a density field, allowing for the direct numerical solution of fluid equations. Data analysis was performed using Python, facilitating the interpretation of simulation outcomes and the identification of key dynamic behaviors.
