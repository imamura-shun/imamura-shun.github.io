Here is the derivation of J.R.Blake's work in 1971, focusing on calculating Green's functions for velocity and pressure fields near interfaces (assumed always flat) between a gas (assumed ideal gas) and an incompressible viscous fluid. The method involves:

  1. Setting boundary conditions at the interface
  2. Using the mirror image method to place opposite forces symmetrically across the interface, with correction terms
  3. Solving the fluid equations via Fourier transform to get a special solution with unknown coefficients
  4. Determining constraints on the correction terms to satisfy boundary conditions
  5. Finding the special solution coefficients from boundary conditions
  6. Inverse Fourier transforming the special solution back to real space
  7. Obtaining the Green's functions

The mirror image method simplifies the calculations. Though perhaps not strictly necessary, it was an innovative technique introduced by Blake to make the problem more tractable.

Original paper:
